# Icon Design Patterns — Geometric Archetypes & Composition

This reference covers the core geometric building blocks, composition rules, and common
icon archetypes for logo marks. Use this when designing Abstract, Corporate, or any
logo style that includes a standalone icon.

---

## Core Principle

Great icon marks are built from simple geometric primitives arranged with intention.
Complexity comes from thoughtful composition, not from adding more shapes.

**The 5-Shape Rule:** If your icon uses more than 5 distinct shapes, it's too complex.
Simplify.

---

## The 7 Geometric Primitives

All iconic marks are built from these:

1. **Circle** — Unity, completeness, global, infinite
2. **Square / Rectangle** — Stability, structure, foundation, reliability
3. **Triangle** — Direction, motion, hierarchy, peak/achievement
4. **Line** — Connection, path, division, flow
5. **Arc / Crescent** — Growth, movement, phase, dynamic
6. **Polygon (5+ sides)** — Complexity, facets, precision, technical
7. **Organic Curve** — Human, natural, fluid, approachable

---

## Composition Strategies

### 1. Overlap (Intersection)
Two or more shapes overlap, creating new forms in the intersection or union.

**Examples:**
- Two circles overlapping = Venn diagram (partnership, intersection)
- Three triangles overlapping at center = star burst (energy, convergence)
- Square + circle overlap = rounded-corner hybrid (tech + human)

**Prompt pattern:**
```
Two [shape 1] and one [shape 2] overlapping at [position]. The intersection
forms a [resulting shape], representing [brand value]. Clean geometric edges.
```

---

### 2. Rotation / Radial Symmetry
A single shape repeated and rotated around a center point.

**Examples:**
- Three crescents rotated 120° apart = dynamic spiral (motion, cycle)
- Four rectangles rotated 90° apart = pinwheel (innovation, energy)
- Six triangles radiating from center = sunburst (radiance, authority)

**Prompt pattern:**
```
[Number] [shapes] rotated evenly around a central point, [degrees] apart.
The composition forms a [overall shape], suggesting [brand value]. Radial
symmetry, balanced, geometric.
```

---

### 3. Nesting (Concentric)
Shapes contained within shapes, like rings or layers.

**Examples:**
- Three concentric circles = target, focus, layers of service
- Square within square within square = depth, foundation, structure
- Triangle within circle = precision within unity

**Prompt pattern:**
```
[Number] concentric [shapes], each [percentage] smaller than the outer.
The layered composition suggests [brand value — e.g., depth, focus, growth].
Clean spacing between each layer.
```

---

### 4. Subtraction (Negative Space)
A large shape with a smaller shape removed, creating a carved-out effect.

**Examples:**
- Circle with triangle removed from top = mountain horizon
- Square with circle punched out = frame, window, focus
- Hexagon with smaller hexagon removed = ring, boundary

**Prompt pattern:**
```
A [large shape] with a [smaller shape] removed from [position — e.g., the
center / the top third / the lower right]. The carved-out space forms a
[resulting icon], representing [brand value]. Clean geometric edges.
```

---

### 5. Reflection / Symmetry
A shape mirrored horizontally or vertically.

**Examples:**
- Two triangles mirrored vertically = hourglass, balance
- Two arcs mirrored horizontally = parentheses, embrace, containment
- Two crescents facing each other = eye, focus, vision

**Prompt pattern:**
```
Two [shapes] mirrored [horizontally / vertically] across a [axis]. The
composition forms a [overall shape], suggesting [brand value]. Perfect
symmetry, geometric precision.
```

---

### 6. Stacking / Layering
Shapes stacked or layered with slight offset, creating depth or progression.

**Examples:**
- Three horizontal bars of descending width = hierarchy, data, growth
- Four squares stacked with 15° rotation = dynamic, innovation
- Five arcs layered = waves, signal, broadcast

**Prompt pattern:**
```
[Number] [shapes] stacked [vertically / horizontally / at an angle], each
[percentage] [smaller / offset / rotated]. The layered composition suggests
[brand value — e.g., growth, signal, progression]. Clean spacing.
```

## Common Icon Archetypes

### Abstract Archetypes

**The Spiral**
- Composition: Arcs or crescents rotated around a center
- Meaning: Growth, evolution, infinite, dynamic
- Industries: Tech, innovation, consulting

**The Shield**
- Composition: Pentagon or chevron shape, often with inner detail
- Meaning: Protection, trust, security, strength
- Industries: Finance, cybersecurity, insurance

**The Star / Burst**
- Composition: Triangles or lines radiating from center
- Meaning: Excellence, energy, radiance, achievement
- Industries: Awards, energy, premium services

**The Knot / Infinity**
- Composition: Overlapping loops or ribbons
- Meaning: Connection, infinite, partnership, continuity
- Industries: Partnerships, consulting, community

**The Diamond**
- Composition: Rotated square or four triangles meeting at center
- Meaning: Value, precision, premium, clarity
- Industries: Luxury, finance, premium goods

**The Wave / Flow**
- Composition: Layered arcs or organic curves
- Meaning: Motion, fluidity, growth, natural
- Industries: Water, wellness, creativity

---

### Pictorial Archetypes (Simplified)

When using recognizable objects, reduce them to geometric essentials:

**The Mountain**
- Geometry: Single triangle or three triangles of varying height
- Simplify: No texture, no shading — pure silhouette

**The Tree**
- Geometry: Triangle (canopy) + rectangle (trunk)
- Simplify: No branches, no leaves — iconic shape only

**The House**
- Geometry: Square (base) + triangle (roof)
- Simplify: No windows unless essential to negative space

**The Bird**
- Geometry: Two arcs (wings) + small circle (head)
- Simplify: No feathers, no details — flying silhouette only

**The Leaf**
- Geometry: Organic teardrop with center vein line
- Simplify: Single smooth curve, one accent line

## Composition Rules

### Rule 1: Optical Balance Over Mathematical Center
Shapes don't always look centered when they're mathematically centered. Triangles and
crescents need slight optical adjustment.

**Example:** A triangle pointing up should sit slightly lower than mathematical center
to feel balanced.

---

### Rule 2: Consistent Stroke Weight
If using outlines, all strokes should be the same width unless contrast is intentional.

**Weak:** Random stroke widths (2px, 4px, 3px)
**Strong:** Uniform 3px strokes throughout

---

### Rule 3: Grid Alignment
All shapes should align to an invisible grid. Corners, edges, and centers should snap
to consistent intervals.

**Describe as:** "All elements aligned to a geometric grid, precise spacing"

---

### Rule 4: Negative Space = Positive Space
The empty space around and within shapes is as important as the shapes themselves.

**Weak:** Shapes crammed together, no breathing room
**Strong:** Generous negative space, each shape has territory

---

### Rule 5: Limit Angles
Stick to 15°, 30°, 45°, 60°, 90°, 120° angles. Random angles (23°, 67°) feel arbitrary.

---

## Size & Scalability

**The Favicon Test:** Will this icon be recognizable at 16x16px?

If no:
- Remove fine details
- Increase stroke weight
- Simplify overlapping shapes
- Reduce number of elements

**The Billboard Test:** Will this icon hold up at 20 feet tall?

If no:
- Add intentional detail or texture
- Ensure shapes have enough weight to not disappear

## Prompt Patterns for Common Requests

### "Make it feel tech / innovative"
```
Geometric, sharp angles (45° or 60°), overlapping shapes suggesting circuitry
or data flow. Crisp edges. Feels precise and forward-looking. Colors: electric
blue and dark gray.
```

### "Make it feel trustworthy / professional"
```
Symmetric composition, balanced, restrained. Simple geometric shapes (shield,
hexagon, or concentric circles). No decorative elements. Feels authoritative
and stable. Colors: navy and gold.
```

### "Make it feel natural / organic"
```
Organic curves, leaf or wave shapes, flowing rather than geometric. Soft edges.
Feels human and approachable. Colors: earth green and warm beige.
```

### "Make it feel bold / energetic"
```
Dynamic angles, rotated shapes, radial burst. Strong contrast. Feels active
and confident. Colors: bright red and black.
```

---

## Final Checklist

Before finalizing an icon mark:

- [ ] Uses 5 or fewer distinct shapes
- [ ] All shapes are geometric primitives or simple combinations
- [ ] Composition strategy is clear (overlap, rotation, nesting, etc.)
- [ ] Negative space is intentional and generous
- [ ] Passes the favicon test (recognizable at 16px)
- [ ] Angles are consistent (15°, 30°, 45°, etc.)
- [ ] Optical balance feels right (not just mathematical center)
- [ ] Stroke weights are uniform (if using outlines)
